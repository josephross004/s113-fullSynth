Etymology 1[edit]

\\n

From super- (prefix), from Middle English super-, from Latin super-, from super (\\u201cabove\\u201d), from Pre-Italic or Proto-Indo-European *eks-uper, from *e\\u1e31s (\\u201cout of\\u201d) (English ex-), from *h\\u2081e\\u01f5\\u02b0s + *uperi (English over). Cognate to hyper, from Ancient Greek.\\n

Adverb[edit]

\\n

super (not comparable)\\n

\\n
  1. (informal) Very; extremely (used like the prefix super-).\\n
    The party was super awesome.
    \\n
    • 1992 March 14, The Canberra Times, page 9, column 2:
      The job is super interesting for a person who enjoys a hardware environment and communicating with people.
\\n

Etymology 2[edit]

\\n

Abbreviation by shortening.\\n

Noun[edit]

\\n

super (plural supers)\\n

\\n
  1. (Australia, New Zealand, informal) Short for superannuation.\\n
    Jane looked forward to collecting a large super payout when she retired.
    2. \\n
  2. Short for supercomputer.\\n
    • 1989, Kai Hwang, Doug DeGroot, Parallel processing for supercomputers and artificial intelligence\\n
      The performances and cost ranges of three classes of commercial supercomputers are given in Table 2.1. The full-scale supers are the most expensive class, represented by Cray, ETA, and Fujitsu systems, for example.
    3. \\n
  3. (comics, slang) Short for superhero.\\n
    • For quotations using this term, see Citations:super.
    4. \\n
  4. (beekeeping) Short for superhive.\\n
    • 1983, Sue Hubbell, A Country Year: Living the Questions, Boston, MA: Mariner Books, published 1999, \\u2192ISBN, page 69:
      There may be thirty to fifty supers in every outyard, and we have only about half an hour to get them off the hives, stacked and covered before the bees get really cross about what we are doing.
    5. \\n
  5. (informal, US) Short for superintendent, especially, a building\'s resident manager (sometimes clarified as \\u201cbuilding super\\u201d).
    6. \\n
  6. (neologism) Short for supernaturalist, especially as distinguished from bright.\\n
    • For quotations using this term, see Citations:super.
    7. \\n
  7. Short for supernumerary; (theater) specifically, a supernumerary actor.\\n
    • 1905, Baroness Emmuska Orczy, chapter 2, in The Affair at the Novelty Theatre‎[1]:
      For this scene, a large number of supers are engaged, and in order to further swell the crowd, practically all the available stage hands have to \\u2018walk on\\u2019 dressed in various coloured dominoes, and all wearing masks.
      • \\n
    • 1916, Ring W. Lardner, \\u201cThree Kings and a Pair\\u201d, in The Saturday Evening Post‎[2]:
      The piece was gave by a bunch o\\u2019 supers the time I went. I\\u2019d like to see it with a real cast. They say it\\u2019s a whiz when it\\u2019s acted right.
    8. \\n
  8. Short for supertanker.\\n
    • 1973, Jeffrey Potter, Disaster by Oil (page 46)\\n
      That is a lot of ship, about the size of big tankers before they grew so rapidly to become supers, mammoths and oilbergs.
    9. \\n
  9. Short for supervisor.

\\n

\\n

Etymology[edit]

\\n

From Proto-Italic *super, from Proto-Indo-European *up\\u00e9r (\\u201cover, above\\u201d). The latter is cognate to Ancient Greek \\u1f51\\u03c0\\u03ad\\u03c1 (hup\\u00e9r, \\u201cabove\\u201d) and Proto-Germanic *uber (English over).\\n

\\n

Pronunciation[edit]

\\n
  • (Classical) IPA(key): /\\u02c8su.per/, [\\u02c8s\\u0320\\u028ap\\u025br]
    • \\n
  • (Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /\\u02c8su.per/, [\\u02c8su\\u02d0per]
\\n

Preposition[edit]

\\n

super (+ accusative, ablative)\\n

\\n
  1. (with accusative) [of place] above, on the top of, upon\\n
    Cibus super mensam est.\\n
    The food is on the table.
    2. \\n
  2. (with accusative) [of place] above, beyond\\n
    • 405 CE, Jerome, Vulgate Genesis.1.2:
      terra autem erat inanis et vacua et tenebrae super faciem abyssi et spiritus Dei ferebatur super aquas
      And the earth was void and empty, and darkness was upon the face of the deep; and the spirit of God moved over the waters.
    3. \\n
  3. (with accusative) [of measure] above, beyond, over, in addition to
    4. \\n
  4. (with ablative) concerning, regarding
\\n
